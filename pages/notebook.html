I may be a balloon. March 31 2025 7:20PM

Inflating it with breath and seeing from the internal space, it’s about pressure, tension, explosion, threshold, fear, anxiety, uncertainty, indeterminacy, chance, risk. At least I have myself, even though I’ve never figured out what that is.

Holding the breath and seeing from the external space, it’s about expanding, reaching out, connecting, vibrating, floating, nomading, wandering. It’s having a conversation with air, wind, water, ground, any surface, and the people around me. We know the electricity on a balloon’s surface when friction happens. I meet people—we hang out, we talk, we exchange, we repel, we attract. I’ve never been as close as the distance between two electrons, side by side. I’ve never been hugged so tightly.
